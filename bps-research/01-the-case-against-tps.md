abstract: |
  Traditional Productivity Systems (TPS) treat productivity as purely cognitive—a matter of time management, task execution, and willpower—while systematically ignoring the biological and psychological substrates upon which all performance depends. This paper lays three charges against TPS: biological negligence (ignoring circadian rhythms, sleep debt, and forcing workers to mask natural performance variations), psychological blindness (ignoring homeostasis, trauma, neurodivergence, and interoceptive awareness), and fragmented approach (cherry-picking tools from scattered disciplines while ignoring foundational systems). These failures are not incidental—they are structural. TPS designs systems for an imaginary user whose biology is constant, psychology is stable, and body requires no consideration.The evidence for an alternative exists. Chronobiology has mapped performance rhythms. Neuroscience has identified biomarkers of peak performance. Psychology has revealed how trauma and nervous system states shape capacity. Yet no integrated field synthesizes these insights. This paper introduces Biorhythmic Productivity Systems (BPS) and the Biopsychosystemic Performance (BPSP) Model, which organises performance drivers into Primary (biological), Secondary (psychological) and Tertiary (systemic) tiers. BPS integrates what TPS fragments, honours what TPS ignores, and collaborates where TPS dominates.This is the opening statement. Subsequent papers will explore the BPSP Model in depth and detail its operationalization. The paradigm is shifting. The revolution has begun.
